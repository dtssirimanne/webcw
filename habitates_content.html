Ocean Habitats

Seagrass Meadows

These meadows are underwater habitats including blooming plants that seed, have roots, and are tethered to the seafloor. The plants generate huge meadows that support a varied range of marine life. Endangered animals such as sea turtles and dugongs eat there. They also house shrimp, fish, and scallops, as well as a variety of other seafood. Scientists feel that destroying these ecosystems is extremely harmful to the general health of the world's seas. The deep sea begins in the aphotic zone, when sunlight loses the majority of its energy to the ocean. Many living forms that exist at these depths are capable of producing their own light, a unique development known as bioluminescence. The deep ocean's water.

Coral Reef

Coral reefs are the most diversified environment on the world. Reefs cover less than one percent of the ocean yet are home to roughly one-quarter of all ocean species. These ancient structures provide an ideal habitat for millions of fish, crabs, clams, starfish, squid, sponges, lobsters, seahorses, sea turtles, and other creatures. Their extraordinary variety has earned them the nickname "rainforests of the sea." Shallow corals enjoy clear, warm, and flowing water in subtropical and tropical oceans.

Kelp Forests

Kelp forests are highly diverse ocean habitats that provide a home and source of food for thousands of marine species. The kelp forests are giant algae that grow in sheltered, underwater forests. They grow at incredible rates, around eighteen inches a day, and are spread out along the west coast of North America.

Deep sea

The deep sea, which is made up of seas deeper than 1,000 meters, accounts for 80 percent of the ocean. Some deep-sea ecosystems may be considered part of the pelagic zone, although the ocean's deepest regions have distinct features. Despite being exceedingly cold, dark, and hostile, a remarkable variety of organisms flourish in this habitat, including several types of jellyfish, the frilled shark, gigantic spider crab, fangtooth fish, six-gill shark, vampire squid, angler fish, and Pacific viperfish.
